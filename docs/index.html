The Bleary-Eyed Bulletin · reporting from the Wreckage · Daily
Sunday 21 June 2026 Matchday · Sat 20 Jun All games settled

Saturday delivered four crackers: Netherlands 5–1 Sweden (Gakpo and Summerville ran riot), Germany 2–1 Ivory Coast (Undav bagged a brace), Ecuador 0–0 Curaçao (a goalless slog that will have delighted precisely nobody), and Tunisia 0–4 Japan (Ueda and Kamada dismantling the Tunisians with gleeful efficiency). Plenty of points on the board — and a fair few managers left staring at the ceiling wondering how they managed to miss all of it.

1stAnamaduwa Athletic · Tom118 pts

Curry-Fuelled Masterclass: Tomo's Japan-Dutch Double Act Fires Him Clear

Wherever Tom is right now — and frankly it could be a beach bar in Koh Samui or a night bus in Osaka — he'll be waking up to a very pleasing screen. Ayase Ueda was absolutely sensational, bagging a brace and an assist in Japan's 4–0 demolition of Tunisia for 18 points. Daichi Kamada chipped in with 11, and Hiroki Ito added a solid 9 from the back. Over in Amsterdam, Alexander Bernhardsson and Viktor Györkeres contributed 1 and 3 respectively as Sweden got walloped 5–1 — not ideal, but the Japanese haul more than papered over those cracks. Tom's day total: a thumping 42 points. He was already 17 clear at the top, and now he's sitting on 118 — a lead so comfortable he could eat a curry, lose his passport and still be fine. Wigs is 17 behind in second. Enjoy the view, Tomo. Try not to get it on your shirt.
