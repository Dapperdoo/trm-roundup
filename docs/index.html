The Bleary-Eyed Bulletin · reporting from the Wreckage · Daily
Sunday 21 June 2026 Matchday · Sat 20 Jun All games settled

Saturday delivered four crackers: Netherlands 5–1 Sweden was the day's showpiece, Cody Gakpo and Crysencio Summerville running riot in orange; Germany 2–1 Côte d'Ivoire was tighter but Deniz Undav bagged a brace for the winners; Tunisia 0–4 Japan was a masterclass in clinical finishing, Ayase Ueda helping himself to a double; and Ecuador 0–0 Curaçao was the kind of goalless draw that fantasy managers have nightmares about. Plenty of hauls, plenty of heartbreak — and a few managers who will be staring at their squads over their morning coffee wondering exactly what went wrong.

Tomo's Japanese and Dutch masterclass fires him further clear

Tom sits pretty at the top on 118, and Saturday gave him every reason to keep smiling from whichever curry house he's currently inhabiting. Ayase Ueda was absolutely magnificent — 18 points from a brace and an assist as Japan dismantled Tunisia 4–0, and at £8.8m he's the bargain of the tournament so far. The only mild grumble: Shogo Taniguchi and Takefusa Kubo both drew blanks. Still, Tom hauled roughly 42 points from Saturday's games alone. The gap to second place is now 17 points. At this rate, Wigs is going to need a very long ladder — and Tom's already eaten the bottom rungs with his bare hands.
