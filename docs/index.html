THE MORNING AFTER

The Bleary-Eyed Bulletin · reporting from the Wreckage · Daily
Saturday 20 June 2026 Matchday · Fri 19 Jun All games settled

Friday night at the World Cup and it was a proper feast: USA 2–0 Australia opened the evening with a clean sheet and a brace of goals, Scotland 0–1 Morocco sent the Tartan Army home early again, Brazil 3–0 Haiti was the Samba show everyone expected, and Turkey 0–1 Paraguay rounded off a night that was very kind to those with Moroccan, Brazilian and American assets — and very unkind to anyone who'd backed Scotland or Turkey to do anything useful. Four games, four results, and a leaderboard that's starting to take shape.

1stLook at his face. Just Look at his FACE! · Sam90 pts

Dest Delivers at the Top — Troughts Stays in the Penthouse

Sam 'Troughts' — the Bard himself — sits pretty at the summit on 90 points, and after Friday's haul of 24 he's got every right to belt out a soliloquy about it. The man of the moment was Sergiño Dest, who romped to a magnificent 12 points as the USA kept a clean sheet and Dest did everything but sell the programmes. Brahim Díaz chipped in a classy 8 points as Morocco dispatched Scotland without breaking a sweat. Ugurcan Çakir and Ferdi Kadioglu scraped 2 each from Turkey's losing effort — which is about as much as you'd expect from a team that couldn't score in a phone box. Sam leads the league. He knows it. He's probably already written a sonnet about it. Insufferable.
