THE MORNING AFTER

The Bleary-Eyed Bulletin · reporting from the Wreckage · Daily
Monday 22 June 2026 Matchday · Sun 21 Jun All games settled

Sunday served up four fixtures and a very uneven spread of fantasy gold. Spain dismantled Saudi Arabia 4–0, handing a feast to anyone holding La Roja, while Belgium and Iran played out a goalless draw that still rewarded the right defenders. Uruguay and Cape Verde served up a wild 2–2, and Egypt edged New Zealand 3–1, with Mohamed Salah doing exactly what it says on the tin. The day's hauls ranged from a very tidy 27 down to a pair of absolute zeroes — and you can probably guess who one of those belongs to.

1stAnamaduwa Athletic · Tom118 pts

Doku Delivers Nothing — Athletic Somehow Still Top

Tom is still top of the league, which is either a testament to his earlier brilliance or a sign that everyone else is also a bit of a shambles. Yesterday, though, was a write-off: Jérémy Doku was his sole representative across all four fixtures and the Belgian winger contributed a grand total of zero points in the goalless draw with Iran. Nil. Nought. A blank so complete it could double as Tom's understanding of what timezone he's currently in. The good news is that his 118-point cushion at the summit held firm — nobody caught him — so he can go back to his curry and his dance music and pretend Sunday never happened. Still, one man, zero points. Even by the standards of someone who's never entirely sure which country he's in, that's a thin return.

2ndDyer's Rusty 9 Iron · Nick115 pts

Yamal and Porro Fire Nick Into the Hunt

While Tom was watching Doku do absolutely nothing, Nick was raking in 15 points and breathing down his neck. Pedro Porro and Lamine Yamal both bagged 7 points apiece as Spain put four past Saudi Arabia without breaking sweat, and Romelu Lukaku chipped in a single point from the Belgium stalemate. It's not the most glamorous haul, but it's enough to keep Nick firmly in second on 115, just three points off the summit. Tom will have clocked that over his shoulder. Nick's tactical eye served him well here — two Spain defenders in a 4–0 win is the sort of thing that looks obvious in hindsight and genius on the morning after. Don't tell Tom.

Trossy Ginge Rockets Up the Table With a 24-Point Sunday

Now then. Dave had a very good Sunday indeed.
