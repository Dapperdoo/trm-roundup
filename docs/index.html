THE MORNING AFTER

The Bleary-Eyed Bulletin · reporting from the Wreckage · Daily
Monday 22 June 2026 Matchday · Sun 21 Jun All games settled

Sunday served up four fixtures and a very Spanish afternoon: ESP 4–0 KSA was the day's centrepiece, a Lamine Yamal-and-Oyarzabal masterclass that sent fantasy points cascading across the league. BEL 0–0 IRN was the dour companion piece — clean sheets for the defenders, crumbs for everyone else. URU 2–2 CPV provided the drama, Maxi Araújo bagging big for the brave few who trusted him, while NZL 1–3 EGY gave Mohamed Salah the stage he never really needs an invitation for. The day's hauls ranged from a tidy 27 down to a pair of goose eggs — and as ever, the gap between genius and disaster was exactly one transfer window of better decisions.

1stAnamaduwa Athletic · Tom118 pts

Doku Delivers Precisely Nothing

Tom is still top of the league — which is either a testament to his Round 1 brilliance or a warning that the wheels are about to come off somewhere over South-East Asia. Sunday was not kind to Anamaduwa Athletic: Jérémy Doku started, played, and contributed a grand total of zero points in Belgium's goalless slog against Iran. That's it. That's the whole entry. Zero from one. The DJ who never knows which country he's in picked a Belgian winger for a match that finished 0–0 and got exactly what the scoreline promised. Still, he stays top — for now. Nick is only three points behind and closing fast, and if Tom's currently somewhere eating a curry with his hands and hasn't checked the app, somebody should probably tell him the pack is coming.
