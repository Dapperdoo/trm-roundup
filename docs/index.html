THE MORNING AFTER

The Bleary-Eyed Bulletin · reporting from the Wreckage · Daily
Sunday 21 June 2026 Matchday · Sat 20 Jun All games settled

Saturday 20 June served up four crackers: Netherlands 5–1 Sweden was the pick of the bunch, a Dutch demolition job that made fantasy managers with Oranje assets very happy indeed; Germany 2–1 Côte d'Ivoire was tighter but still delivered goals and points; Tunisia 0–4 Japan was a four-star shellacking that sent the Samurai Blue's owners dancing; and Ecuador 0–0 Curaçao was the dour, goalless stalemate nobody wanted but a few defenders quietly appreciated. Plenty of big scores, a couple of horror shows, and one Swedish defender who somehow managed to go negative. Saturday gave and Saturday took away.

1stAnamaduwa Athletic · Tom118 pts

Ueda, Kamada, Ito — Tomo's Japanese invasion pays off handsomely

Somewhere between a curry and a DJ set, Tom has stumbled into first place and shows absolutely no sign of leaving. Saturday's haul was built on Japan's four-goal rout of Tunisia: Ayase Ueda was magnificent with 18 points, Daichi Kamada weighed in with 11, and Hiroki Ito added 9 at the back. Sweden chipped in too — Viktor Györkeres got 3 and Alexander Bernhardsson 1 — giving Tomo a Saturday total of 42 points from the day's games. That's the kind of return that makes a man feel smug even when he can't remember which continent he's currently eating a curry on. He leads the league on 118, a full 17 clear of Wigs in second. Whether Tom knows that or not is another question entirely.
