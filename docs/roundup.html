Pépé takes a bow, Sam holds the stage

Twenty-two points and the lead intact — Sam will take that. The supporting cast did their bit: clean-sheet twos apiece from Hernán Galíndez, Joel Ordóñez and Joshua Kimmich, with Tsuyoshi Watanabe and Sergiño Dest chipping in singles. Matt Freese didn't feature. It's no vintage haul, but like a long Shakespearean fifth act it gets the job done without anyone walking out early. Top of the table, pint in hand and brother Wigs creeping up the order — Sam keeps the spotlight for another day.

Doué does the heavy lifting

Tom's Japanese bloc nicks a baker's dozen

Still chasing that maddeningly elusive first title; still close enough to taste it.

Van Dijk keeps Joe S ticking over

A modest ten for Joe S, anchored — fittingly for a defender's defender — by Virgil van Dijk's five and an assist in the Dutch win. 5th50 Shades of O'Shea · Wigs164 pts

Wigs banks a tidy nineteen

A healthy nineteen for Wigs, and the pick of it came from the unglamorous end. Calm, measured and steadily accumulating, Wigs is doing exactly what he does best: turning up, saying little, and wandering off with the points.
